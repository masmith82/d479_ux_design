About Our Island

History and Culture

Taniti has long been a meeting point for seafarers. Fishing villages grew into a bustling harbor town, blending indigenous traditions with influences from traders across the Pacific. The island now celebrates a lively calendar of dance, canoe racing, and artisan markets.

Visitors can explore heritage sites, learn traditional weaving, and sample coastal cuisine centered on fresh fruit, citrus, and spice blends unique to the island.

Miscellany and FAQ

What is the drinking age?

Guests must be 21 or older to purchase alcohol.

What kind of power outlets are available?

Taniti uses standard 120V outlets (Type A and B).

What currency is accepted?

Taniti Dollars (TND) and major credit cards are widely used.

Is the island safe for travelers?

Yes. The island is walkable, and local guides are available for all excursions.

Do I need reservations for tours?

We recommend booking popular tours 2-4 weeks ahead, especially in June and December.
